What's it do?
It makes threads of tweets, so you can send a full conversation to someone else (or include it in another tweet) to people rather than copying and pasting half a dozen different URLs for each one.
Why did you make Thweddy?
It looked like a fun idea to implement, other solutions didn't seem up to scratch, and I like a challenge. Not to mention it itches my own scratch regards sending a conversation, made up of individual tweets, to other people.
Can I look up threads from the first tweet?
Unfortunately no, this is because you literally can't do this using the Twitter API at the moment, but this will hopefully change in version 2.
Doesn't Twonvo already do this?
Yes, Twonvo does attempt to solve the same problem, but I personally feel Thweddy is a nicer solution, I have fun working on it, and it's free as in speech.
Why do I need to login to Twitter?
Thweddy doesn't store your login credentials, or any login credentials, so we need a way to tie threads back to you. Also some tweets are private on twitter and only viewable if allowed by the author, in which case we need to use your login to access them.
It broke!
Well now, you can either tell me about and I'll try to get round to fixing it, or you can fix it yourself.
Can I install Thweddy on my server?
Sure, it's open source, you can grab the code and do whatever you like with it.
